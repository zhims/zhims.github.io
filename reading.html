Yao Zhang - Reading


Study widely used deep learning models through a unified mathematical lens, treating eacharchitecture as an explicit input–output mapping and analyzing it at three tightly connected levels. Network-structure expression: represent model families (e.g., MLPs, CNNs, RNNs/LSTMs, Transformers/ViTs, GNNs, and PINNs) as explicit function compositions or operators acting on inputs, highlighting the central inductive mechanisms—convolution for locality, recurrence for temporal dependence, self-attention for global interaction, message passing for relational structure, and physics-based constraints for scientific consistency. Loss-function expression (with architecture embedded): formulate objectives directly in terms of network parameters and the representations produced by the forward pass, combining task losses with regularization and, when appropriate, structure-aware or physics-informed penalty terms. Optimization strategy: derive parameter updates via backpropagation and automatic differentiation, then summarize practical training choices such as SGD or Adam-type optimizers, learning-rate schedules, normalization schemes, and common stabilization techniques that ensure efficient and robust convergence. Applications in science and engineering: deep learning models approximate complex high-dimensional relationships for real-world problems. They support surrogate modeling for PDEs, data-driven discovery in scientific systems, and accelerated simulation in areas such as astrophysics, while enabling perception, prediction, and control in engineering applications including computer vision, robotics, biomedical engineering, and infrastructure forecasting.
